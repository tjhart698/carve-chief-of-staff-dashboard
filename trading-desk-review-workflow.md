# Trading Desk Review Workflow

Use this workflow whenever Tyler asks for a stock or ETF trade recommendation.

## Governing Documents
- `chief-of-staff/trading-desk-charter.md`
- `trading/trading-mandate.md`
- `trading/investing-thesis-2026-05-07.md`
- `trading/current-holdings.md`
- `trading/research/YYYY-MM-DD.md` when available

## Workflow

## Standard Templates
- Bull Analyst: `chief-of-staff/trading-desk-bull-analyst-template.md`
- Bear Analyst: `chief-of-staff/trading-desk-bear-analyst-template.md`
- Market Chief: `chief-of-staff/trading-desk-market-chief-template.md`
- Tyler-facing final memo: `chief-of-staff/trading-desk-final-recommendation-template.md`


### Step 1 — Charter check
Before analysis begins, confirm:
- instrument is allowed
- idea fits the project sleeve
- concentration and sizing limits are respected
- the setup does not violate execution or risk rules

If the idea fails the charter check, recommend **PASS** immediately.

### Step 2 — Bull Analyst memo
Bull Analyst prepares:
- setup summary
- upside thesis
- catalysts
- why now
- expected reward / asymmetry
- what success looks like

### Step 3 — Bear Analyst memo
Bear Analyst prepares:
- downside thesis
- hidden risks
- macro / valuation / liquidity / execution concerns
- what could break
- what would invalidate the trade

### Step 4 — Market Chief decision
Market Chief reviews both and produces one final call:
- **YES** = approval-ready recommendation
- **WAIT** = interesting but not ready / wrong entry / incomplete edge
- **PASS** = should not be taken under the current mandate

### Step 5 — Tyler-facing output
Use `chief-of-staff/trading-desk-final-recommendation-template.md`.

The final memo to Tyler should include:
- ticker
- action
- bull case summary
- bear case summary
- final recommendation
- why now
- position size
- entry method
- invalidation / exit logic
- portfolio impact
- preflight result
- whether Tyler approval is requested

## Non-Negotiables
- No trade is submitted without Tyler approval
- No data is invented
- No rule is relaxed because the upside sounds attractive
- If the bull and bear cases are both weak or unclear, the answer is **PASS** or **WAIT**, not forced activity
