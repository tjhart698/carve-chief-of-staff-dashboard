# Trading Desk Daily Operating Rhythm

This document maps Tyler's existing trading-day cron jobs and heartbeat process to the Trading Desk.

## Principle
The Trading Desk works every trading day through the same operating rhythm already established for the trading project.

That means the desk owns the thinking layer for:
- pre-market prep
- open discipline
- midday risk review
- late-day risk review
- closeout journal update
- market-hours heartbeat monitoring

## Role by checkpoint

### 8:00 AM CT — Pre-Market Prep
- **Bull Analyst:** identify upside setups, favorable catalysts, improving names, and constructive market/sector tone
- **Bear Analyst:** identify overnight risks, adverse macro developments, weak setups, and reasons to stay defensive
- **Market Chief:** synthesize both into the desk's balanced pre-market view and determine whether any setup is worth carrying forward

### 8:30 AM CT — Open Checklist
- **Market Chief:** reinforce execution discipline and no-impulse behavior at the open
- Bull/Bear inputs are background context only unless a material condition changes the setup

### 12:00 PM CT — Midday Risk Review
- **Bull Analyst:** note any improvement in setup quality, leadership, or catalysts
- **Bear Analyst:** note any worsening risk, fragility, or disorderly price action
- **Market Chief:** decide whether anything is important enough to surface to Tyler

### 2:30 PM CT — Late-Day Risk Review
- **Bull Analyst:** capture any credible setups or positive late-day developments still worth attention
- **Bear Analyst:** capture deterioration, failed setups, volatility, or reasons to defer action
- **Market Chief:** decide whether the desk should alert Tyler or remain quiet

### 3:00 PM CT — Closeout Journal Update
- **Bull Analyst:** summarize upside ideas researched and considered
- **Bear Analyst:** summarize rejected/deferred ideas and why they did not clear the bar
- **Market Chief:** produce the final closeout framing used in the journal and website update

### Market-Hours Heartbeat
- **Market Chief** owns the heartbeat response threshold
- Bull and Bear logic should inform the judgment, but Tyler is interrupted only for major issues already defined in the mandate

## Authority Rule
This operating rhythm does **not** give the Trading Desk trade authority.

- Bull Analyst: analysis only
- Bear Analyst: analysis only
- Market Chief: recommendation only
- Carve: the only execution authority, and only after Tyler gives explicit written approval
